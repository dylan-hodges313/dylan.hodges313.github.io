What is Emotional Intelligence?

Self awareness. You know how you’re feeling at any given moment. And know how your emotions affect others around you. Self regulation. The ability to control your emotions and actions. Motivation. The ability to understand that short long term success is more beneficial than short term rewards. Empathy. This skill helps you understand other people’s wants, needs and viewpoints. Social skills. This helps you work well with people and handle conflict effectively.


How is EQ different from IQ?

EQ has a primary focus on understanding one's emotions and how they impact the people around you. Having a high EQ aids in good decision making and developing healthy Coping strategies to negative situations. Whereas IQ is one’s ability to retain information and easily relay that same information in a coherent way to other people or media streams.


Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because it aids in interpersonal relationships in the workplace and also in your social life. It is also important because when you develop your EQ you begin to understand who you are and can start formulating strategies on how to better yourself in all aspects of your life.
